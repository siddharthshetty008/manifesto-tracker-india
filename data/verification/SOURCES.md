## Source Reliability Classification

| Category | Examples | How Used |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Primary Government** | PIB, MoD notifications, Gazette of India, Parliament records | Revenue figures, dates, policy provisions — used as authoritative facts |
| **Constitutional/Legal** | Supreme Court judgments, Constitutional Amendment Acts | Legal holdings, dates — treated as definitive |
| **Non-Partisan Think Tanks** | PRS India, FactChecker.in | Analysis, budget data — used for context and independent validation |
| **Media** | Business Standard, LiveLaw, Outlook | Court proceedings reporting, quotes — used for context, cross-referenced |

---

## Data Integrity Notes

1. **Revenue figures** are from PIB official press releases — the government's own published data
2. **Legislative dates** cross-referenced between PRS India and PIB
3. **Court holdings** sourced from SC Observer and LiveLaw (both carry full judgment text)
4. **Compensation figures** from PIB (PRID-1829777) and Finance Minister's public statement
5. **Taxpayer count** from PIB's record collection press note (as of April 30, 2025)
6. Where data comes from **media sources**, it supplements (not replaces) government data
7. All Q&A pairs cite sources inline so the model learns to attribute claims
